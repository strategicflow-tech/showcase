We've completely redesigned Rize Settings to make managing your organization, teams, and billing simpler and more intuitive. What was once scattered across separate team and individual settings is now unified in one clean, organized interface that puts everything you need right at your fingertips.
What's New
  • Unified Settings Hub: Access all your settings from a single entry point at the bottom of your sidebar. No more confusion between team and individual settings.
  • Better Organization: Settings are now logically grouped into sections including Account, Billing, Organization, and individual Workspace tabs for easy navigation.
  • Organization Members Tab: A dedicated view showing all members in your organization with filtering by account, role, and status. See exactly which teams each member belongs to at a glance.
  • Workspace-Specific Controls: Manage members, enable profitability tracking, and toggle billable time directly from each workspace's settings.
  • Improved Billing Visibility: Clearly see your active seat count and how it connects to your subscription, making billing management transparent and straightforward.
  • Quick Search: Find any setting instantly using keyword search across all settings categories.
How It Works
  • 01
    Click the Settings link at the bottom of your sidebar to access the new unified settings interface.
  • 02
    Navigate between Account, Billing, Organization, and individual workspace tabs to find what you need.
  • 03
    Visit the Organization Members tab to view all members, filter by various criteria, and manage team assignments in one place.
  • 04
    Click into any workspace to edit members, configure integrations, access API information, or enable profitability and billable time tracking.
  • 05
    Use the search bar at the top to quickly jump to any specific setting by keyword.

You were managing organization members in one tab, checking billing in another, and configuring workspace features somewhere else entirely. Every setting change meant a scavenger hunt — and if you needed to see which team a member belonged to, you were opening a support ticket or cross-referencing a spreadsheet.

Rize rebuilt its entire settings architecture into a single hub at the bottom of your sidebar. Organization, billing, workspaces — one entry point, logical grouping, instant keyword search. The Organization Members tab now shows every user's team assignments and roles at a glance, with filtering by account type, role, and status.

Teams that haven't updated yet are still reconciling seat counts manually and toggling profitability settings one workspace at a time. The new workspace-specific controls — billable time, profitability tracking, API access, integrations — are per-workspace now. That means client data stays separate, configurations don't bleed across projects, and you stop configuring the same toggle in six different places.

The 6 upgrades — and why each one works
1 · Subject: company announcement → reader problem resolved
"New Rize Settings" describes what Rize did. "Your settings were in three different places. Now they're not." describes what the reader experienced and what just changed. The reader recognizes the problem before they know what the solution is — which is exactly why they open it.
2 · Opening: "We've completely redesigned" → reader's daily friction named first
The original opens with what Rize accomplished. The rewrite opens with what the reader was doing wrong before this update: managing members in one tab, billing in another, workspace features somewhere else. The reader feels understood before they feel sold to.
3 · Six equal bullets → hierarchy preserved, facts kept
The original gives "Quick Search" the same visual weight as "Organization Members Tab" — a minor convenience treated identically to a major capability. The rewrite names all six features in a dedicated grid, but the body paragraph leads with the highest-value capabilities (Members tab, workspace controls) and saves Quick Search for the grid. Specificity is preserved — no facts were replaced with abstractions.
4 · "How It Works" task list → consequence paragraph instead
The original teaches setup before it earns the right to do so. The rewrite puts the consequence of the old system — cross-referencing spreadsheets, opening support tickets, toggling the same setting six times — in the body where it creates urgency. The how-to lives in the feature grid for readers who are already convinced.
5 · Zero-consequence email → inaction made costly
"Teams that haven't updated yet are still reconciling seat counts manually and toggling profitability settings one workspace at a time." This sentence didn't exist in the original. It names what the reader is still doing wrong — and makes the update feel overdue rather than optional.
6 · Generic CTA → specific action with outcome
"Explore the new settings →" could be any product update from any company. "Open your new settings →" implies they already belong to the reader — the settings exist, they're waiting, the only action is to go there. Ownership framing converts better because it removes the friction of evaluation.
Content Fidelity Rule applied here
All 6 named features from the original (Unified Hub, Members Tab, Workspace Controls, Billing Visibility, Quick Search, Better Organization) appear in the rebuilt version — in the feature grid and in the body. Strategic Flow reframes structure and opening. It never replaces specific product facts with generic abstractions. The information stays. The framing changes.
