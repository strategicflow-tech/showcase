Mailsuite Campaigns turns Gmail into a mass email tool for up to 10,000 recipients

Count on Mailsuite to send your email marketing campaigns to up to 10,000 contacts at once. Without ever leaving your Gmail app.

Sometimes you need to send a mass email to a group of people, such as co-workers, partners, or colleagues. And of course, you want to know if they received your message or not.

That's why we created Mailsuite Campaign, so you can send individual copies of an email to up to 10,000 recipients. That way, you don't need to use an external email marketing tool, nor confuse your contacts by hiding them in your emails' BCC field.

Turning your Gmail into a Marketing Platform for Mass Emails

As with everything else at Mailsuite for Gmail, Mailsuite Campaign is super easy to use. To send individual tracked emails to up to 10,000 recipients:

  1. Hit "Compose" to write your email as you always do — every compose window can be turned into a Campaign message. Click "Campaign" in the top right corner. Your compose window turns green.
  2. Add the email addresses of your recipients — just like any email. Paste up to 10,000 addresses into the "to" field.
  3. Hit send and keep track of them — each recipient gets an individual copy. Track opens and link clicks by hovering over Mailsuite's checkmarks.

Benefits of Mailsuite Campaign

Mailsuite Campaign brings you the best of both worlds: Email marketing functionality and deliverability controls.

  • Hyper-targeted email campaigns with improved deliverability — Gmail limits keep inboxes streamlined. Send through your business domain for better deliverability than shared IP ESPs.
  • No new accounts, passwords, or any other hassle — you never leave Gmail. Send and track as you've always done, now with mass email power.
  • Mailsuite Campaign is in beta — share feedback via Twitter @Mailsuite_

The 7 Strategic Upgrades

What changed, and why each change converts better

Subject line transformation
❌ Original
Mailsuite Campaigns turns Gmail into a mass email tool for up to 10,000 recipients
Product action as headline. Describes what Mailsuite does, not what the reader is losing. Could be skipped by anyone already using an ESP.
✓ Rebuilt
Track 10K emails in real-time from your Gmail
Reader outcome as headline. Names the specific benefit (real-time tracking at scale) and the environment they're already in (Gmail). Zero onboarding anxiety.
Upgrade 01
Problem before product — always
The original opens with what Mailsuite does ("turns Gmail into a mass email tool"). The rebuild opens with what the reader is experiencing: ESP exports, manual reconciliation, hours of waiting for tracking data. The reader recognises their problem in the first sentence — that's what makes them keep reading.
Upgrade 02
10K, 0, Real-time — numbers replace prose
The original mentions 10,000 recipients in the headline and once in the body — but it's embedded in prose. The rebuild surfaces three numbers as stat cards above the fold: 10K recipients, 0 new accounts, real-time tracking. Numbers before features. Always. They're scannable, verifiable, and memorable.
Upgrade 03
Four features unified into one email
The original only covers Campaigns. Real-time notifications, reminders, and Last Read Insights live in separate blog posts. A user who reads the Campaigns post doesn't discover the companion features. The rebuild treats all four as one system: 3 pain points in the hook → 3 solutions + 1 intelligence layer in the cards. Perfect narrative symmetry.
Upgrade 04
Steps removed, outcomes installed
The original walks through three how-to steps (Compose → Add recipients → Send) before stating any benefit. The rebuild removes the steps entirely from the email. Email is not documentation. The job of an email is to create enough desire that the reader clicks through to learn the steps. Steps belong on the landing page, not in the hook.
Upgrade 05
Beta disclaimer → confidence statement
The original includes "Mailsuite Campaign is in beta: send your feedback" as a feature point. Beta signals unfinished. The rebuild replaces it entirely. The footer instead reads "1.5M+ active users" — social proof that makes the product feel established, not experimental.
Upgrade 06
Two CTAs at two conversion moments
The original has no CTA in the email version. The rebuild places CTA 1 above the feature cards ("Track 10K emails in real-time from Gmail") when curiosity peaks after the stat cards. CTA 2 after the Before/After closes with a framing line: "Your Gmail is already a mass email platform. You just haven't turned it on yet." Ownership language — the reader is the agent, not the recipient.
Upgrade 07
8-week content calendar — retention architecture
The original is a one-shot post. The rebuild includes 8 follow-up email topics that each cover a distinct Mailsuite feature, ensuring the full product gets surfaced over 4 weeks. Every feature card in the email becomes its own email later. The launch email is the top of a conversion funnel, not the end of it.

This is the Strategic Flow Method

Problem before product. Numbers before features. Companion features unified into one narrative. Steps stay on the landing page. Beta removed, social proof installed. Two CTAs, two moments. Content calendar turns one email into eight.
