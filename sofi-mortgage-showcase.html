The 6 Strategic Upgrades
1
Consequence before product
"Purchase your dream home" is aspiration. "Rate lock expires. Seller takes a backup offer. You lose the house." is consequence. The rebuild opens with what happens when the buyer uses a slow lender — before any SoFi product is mentioned. Consequence-first creates urgency that no feature claim can replicate.
2
The $10,000 guarantee earns the headline
No traditional bank guarantees their closing timeline with cash. SoFi does. In the original, this appears as bullet #1 with equal visual weight as "low down payment options." In the rebuild, it's fused into the headline itself: "Close 17 days faster — or we pay up to $10,000." One sentence does what four bullets cannot.
3
Lock and Look surfaces as an anxiety-resolver
The fear of rates rising during a home search is real and underserved. SoFi's 90-day Lock and Look directly solves this — but it's buried in a secondary section. Promoted to the second benefit card, it becomes the feature that separates SoFi from every lender who forces buyers to time the market.
4
Rates become an invitation, not a table
4.990% / 5.000% / 5.375% / 5.625% presented as a static rate table creates no action. Reframed with "Updated daily. Your rate may be lower. Soft pull only — 60 seconds." — the same data becomes an invitation to find out your specific rate. The difference between information and conversion is personalization.
5
Stack the savings explicitly
$9,500 cash back + $500 member discount + $500 SoFi Plus discount = potentially $10,500 in savings at close. The original never stacks these. The rebuild presents them together near the CTA where price anxiety is highest — "Stack cash back + member discounts at close" makes the total feel intentional, not accidental.
6
Two CTAs, two conversion moments
The original repeats "View your rate" six times identically. The rebuild uses a conversion ladder: CTA 1 removes fear ("soft pull, no credit impact — 60 seconds") at the top when the reader is curious. CTA 2 creates urgency ("Start my 32-day close") at the bottom when the reader is convinced. Same destination, different psychological moment.
